Insurance cores drown in **volume and variance**: every partner tweaks segments, qualifiers, and timelines. **EDI** gives you a **lingua franca**; a **clearinghouse** mindset gives you **control** before bad data hits policy and finance systems.

## Problem

Raw files arrive from providers and intermediaries. Some batches are huge; some are wrong. Without a dedicated **ingress and filtering** layer, your core spends engineering time on parsing exceptions instead of paying claims correctly.

## Approach

- **Normalize at the edge.** Ingest EDI (or API equivalents), validate against **trading partner agreements**, and persist **canonical internal messages** your domain services understand.
- **Idempotency everywhere.** Replays and duplicate submissions are normal. Keys derived from sender, control numbers, and content hashes prevent double payment and simplify support.
- **Filtering agents as explicit services.** Eligibility checks, duplicate detection, fraud heuristics, and business edits should be **composable pipelines** with clear logs — not one 5,000-line job.
- **Human queues for the last mile.** Automate the 90% case; surface the 10% with **operator tooling**: original payload, rule that fired, override with audit.

## Lessons

- **Observability beats heroics.** Per-partner dashboards (volume, error rate, SLA) turn Friday outages into Tuesday fixes.
- **Version partner maps.** When a payer changes a segment requirement, you need to know **which** mapping version produced a given claim.
- **Core stays boring.** Let the clearinghouse absorb chaos so pricing, benefits, and payment stay deterministic.

This is the shape of the **TillaHealth**-class problem: not “parse a file,” but **run a reliable exchange** between members, providers, and the insurer’s brain.
