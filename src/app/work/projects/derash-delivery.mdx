## Problem

Restaurant delivery in a dense metro must survive **peak dinner hours**: order spikes, driver assignment churn, and customer expectations for live status — all without melting the database or losing money on bad ETAs.

## Tech stack

- **Backend services** for orders, restaurants, drivers, and payouts
- **Realtime** tracking and notification paths
- **Infrastructure** tuned for bursty traffic (autoscaling, caching, queue discipline)

## What I built

- Core **backend and DevOps** for a DMV-area delivery product.
- **Real-time order tracking** with attention to consistency vs. latency tradeoffs.
- **Logistics optimization** primitives: batching, dispatch rules, and failure recovery when drivers go offline mid-route.

## Outcome

A system that could **ride peak load** without becoming operationally fragile — the difference between “works in demo” and “works Friday at 7pm.”
