## Problem

A ministry-aligned ERP SaaS needed predictable delivery across many contributors: clear priorities, reviewable code, and junior engineers growing into ownership — not heroics every sprint.

## Tech stack

- Multi-module **SaaS** architecture (tenant isolation, billing hooks, shared platform services)
- **Agile** ceremonies with emphasis on definition of done and release readiness

## What I built

- **Project lead** responsibilities: roadmap alignment with stakeholders, sprint execution, and risk escalation.
- **Rigorous code reviews** and pairing to keep modules maintainable as the team grew.
- **Mentorship** so less experienced developers could own features with guardrails.

## Outcome

Higher **baseline quality** and calmer releases — less rework, clearer ownership, and a team that could sustain delivery after the initial push.
