## Problem

Healthcare organizations needed a modern, multi-tenant EHR that could exchange clinical data with external systems and partners without locking each hospital into a proprietary silo. Workflows span admissions, encounters, orders, and documents — all while tenants must stay isolated and auditable.

## Tech stack

- **Interoperability:** HL7 FHIR (resources, APIs, versioning strategy)
- **Architecture:** Multi-tenant core, service boundaries aligned to clinical domains
- **Stack:** Full-stack web platform with emphasis on secure APIs and role-based access

## What I built

- System architecture and delivery leadership from inception: tenant model, core clinical modules, and integration surface for FHIR consumers.
- FHIR-aligned modeling so partners could connect standard clients and future national health information exchange scenarios.
- Operational patterns for complex hospital workflows while keeping upgrade paths clear for evolving regulation and profiles.

## Outcome

A path to **global interoperability** by design: clinical data modeled and exposed through **FHIR** instead of one-off integrations, reducing long-term coupling and integration cost for each new facility or vendor.
